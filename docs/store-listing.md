## Full description

Orbit helps you call the people you keep meaning to call.

Most of the friction in staying in touch is deciding who to call — not making the call itself. Orbit removes that step. You organize people into lists based on mood, relationship, or context. Then you open the app, and it hands you one name.

**How it works**

Orbit shows you a card: one person, their photo, when you last talked, how often you usually connect, and which list they came from. Tap to call. Swipe left if now is not the right moment; swipe right if you want to reach out sooner. That's the whole loop.

Your contacts surface in a rhythm shaped by how often you want to stay in touch with each list. A list called "inner orbit" might surface someone every few days. A list called "old friends" might wait a few weeks between suggestions. The app tracks your call history automatically and adjusts without you doing anything.

**Lists**

You can have as many lists as you like: closest friends, people you look up to, people you used to call more, late-night conversations, people in recovery who keep you grounded. Names are yours — Orbit does not suggest how you should think about your relationships.

Each list has a rule: spaced repetition (contact surfaces more often if you call back quickly), round robin (even rotation across everyone), or recency queue (most-overdue first). You can tune the rhythm per list and override it for specific contacts.

**Other features**

- Browse your full list at any time and call anyone directly
- Notes after calls: a short journal entry tied to a contact
- Pause a contact for a week, a month, or indefinitely — they disappear from surfacing without losing their history
- Widgets for your home screen: see who is next without opening the app
- Daily notifications with how many people are due today
- Manual encrypted export of all your data

**Privacy**

Nothing leaves your phone. Orbit reads your call history and contacts to do its job, but that data never goes anywhere. There are no servers, no accounts, no analytics. Everything is encrypted at rest. Uninstall the app and everything is gone.

Orbit is for anyone who has lost touch with people they care about — not because they stopped caring, but because deciding who to call was one step too many.
